Ethiopian Jazz / Vinyl Collecting

Is Ethiopian jazz vinyl worth buying, or should I just get the CDs?

Save your vinyl budget for labels like Awesome Tapes From Africa and Strut Records, where the pressings actually justify the cost. There is also the Ethiopiques series on CD. The owner of Suprimo Records in Baltimore put it plainly: the series spans 30+ volumes and vinyl reissues are expensive.

Why Ethiopiques is a CD-first series

The Ethiopiques series, on French label Buda Musique, is the definitive collection of Ethiopian jazz from the 1960s and 70s. It's how most Western listeners first found Mulatu Astatke and Hailu Mergia. With 30+ volumes, collecting on vinyl gets expensive fast, and the reissues don't add much beyond the format itself.

The music is the point. Start with CDs, stream to find your favorites, then buy selectively on vinyl once you know what you actually want to own.

How to find it on Discogs

Search by artist name or label. Filter by seller rating and condition before looking at price.

  • Labels Search Awesome Tapes From Africa and Strut directly. Both have consistent quality across their catalog.
  • Seller 99% rating or higher. Don't compromise on this.
  • Condition VG+ or better. For reissues especially, there's no reason to accept less.
  • Pressings Original pressings matter less here than in jazz or rock collecting. The reissues are generally the better copies anyway.

One more thing

If you're not sure which Ethiopiques volumes to start with, ask at a local record store. The staff at Suprimo pointed me toward vinyl alternatives instead of pushing a sale, which tells you something about how deep the catalog runs. That kind of advice is worth more than any list.
